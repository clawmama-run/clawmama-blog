# Dev.to Publishing Rules

Dev.to is a distribution channel for selected ClawMama articles. It is not a dumping ground for every blog post.

## Frequency

Default cadence:

- Week 1: 2-3 posts total.
- Week 2: about 3 posts total.
- Week 3 onward: 3-5 posts/week if account health is normal.
- Hard cap: 1 post/day.

If posts receive low engagement, warnings, suspicious account behavior, or moderation friction, reduce cadence immediately.

## What qualifies for Dev.to

A post can be syndicated if it is useful to a reader even without knowing ClawMama.

Good Dev.to candidates:

- practical tutorials;
- checklists;
- plain-English comparisons;
- deployment risk explanations;
- workflow examples for research, sales, marketing, or operations;
- articles that explain OpenClaw/Hermes hosting trade-offs.

Bad Dev.to candidates:

- internal notes;
- release notes with no educational value;
- short SEO stubs;
- repetitive product announcements;
- posts whose only point is “try ClawMama”;
- scaffold/example content.

## Reader-first rewrite checklist

Before publishing to Dev.to, rewrite or review the article with this checklist:

1. Does the first paragraph describe a real job or pain?
2. Would the article still be useful if the reader does not click the ClawMama link?
3. Is the technical detail explained in plain language?
4. Is ClawMama introduced as one practical option, not as spam?
5. Is there only one light CTA near the end?
6. Is the canonical blog URL included?

## CTA style

Use a soft CTA:

> If you want this kind of AI bot without managing a VPS, ClawMama provides Telegram-first managed hosting for OpenClaw and Hermes runtimes.

Avoid repetitive hard CTA language such as:

- “Sign up now!”
- “Best platform ever!”
- “Revolutionary AI agent hosting!”

## Link policy

Recommended maximum per Dev.to post:

- 1 canonical link to `https://blog.clawmama.run/...`
- 1 product link to `https://clawmama.run/` or `https://t.me/clawmamarun_bot`

Do not over-link every paragraph.

## Publishing workflow

1. Publish or update the canonical article on the Astro blog.
2. Wait for GitHub Pages deployment to succeed.
3. Check the public blog URL in BrowserMan if possible.
4. Prepare a Dev.to version with canonical URL and soft CTA.
5. Publish through BrowserMan.
6. Save the Dev.to URL in the daily memory/log.

## Safety rule

External publishing is visible brand activity. If an article is obviously promotional, unreviewed, or low-quality, do not publish it to Dev.to just to hit a number. Blog volume can be high; Dev.to volume must be selective.
