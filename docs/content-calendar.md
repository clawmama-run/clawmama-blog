# ClawMama 30-Day Content Calendar

This calendar is a production plan, not a promise that every post must be syndicated. The blog can publish more frequently because it is ClawMama's own asset. Dev.to should stay selective and low-volume.

## Publishing targets

Minimum daily blog output:

- 2 blog posts per day.

Ideal daily blog output:

- 3 blog posts per day: one workflow/use-case article, one SEO/comparison article, one short FAQ/checklist.

Dev.to output:

- Week 1: 2-3 posts total.
- Week 2: about 3 posts total.
- Week 3 onward: 3-5 posts/week only if account health and engagement look normal.
- Absolute cap: 1 post/day.

## Daily production pattern

Each day should include:

1. Pick the reader persona before drafting.
2. Write blog content around a concrete job-to-be-done.
3. Validate and deploy the blog.
4. Choose at most one article for Dev.to, only if it is genuinely useful outside ClawMama.
5. Record published URLs.

## Article priority score

Before writing, score each idea from 1-5:

- Reader pain: is the problem real and frequent?
- Business fit: does ClawMama naturally help?
- Search intent: might someone search this?
- Dev.to fit: would a developer/operator audience find it useful?
- Conversion fit: could it move a reader toward trying the Telegram bot?

Prioritize posts with high reader pain and business fit. Search volume is useful, but it should not override reader usefulness.
